Learn about the Work Process

As my work is figurative I find traditional methods of working the clay, slabbing and coiling lend themselves to my particular way of modelling. I use a smooth stoneware clay which lets me refine the surface, paying attention to fine detail. This enables me to make original one off pieces as I love the texture, the versatility and plasticity of the material.

Modelling


When modelling a piece of work for a limited edition, I use an armature to form the clay around. This supports the clay while I work on it. This will be removed when I cut the piece up to make a multi piece plaster mould.
Moulds


This photograph shows the multi piece plaster mould.These pieces of moulds are cast separately by pressing slabs of rolled out clay into them and then joining the pieces together. Once all the pieces have been cast they can be removed from the moulds and re assembled as a whole.
Glazing


This shows the piece after it's first fireing. It is being glazed before having up to four fireings in order to get the colours just right.
Finished Sculpture


The finished piece, 'Balu' the french boxer. I like to work to commission, Portrait Busts and Wildlife forming the bulk of my portfolio.
